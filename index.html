Welcome to Muslim Ladies College

Galle Muslim Girls’ College, established in 1958 as Malharussulhiya School at Circular Road, Galle, began as a mixed school providing education in Tamil and English mediums under the leadership of its first principal, Mrs. A. R. Wadud. In 1965, the institution was reorganized as a girls’ school dedicated to Muslim female education and was later relocated to Thalapitiya, Galle.

In 1973, the school was officially renamed Galle Muslim Ladies College. Over the years, the college has expanded both academically and infrastructural, including the introduction of Advanced Level classes in the Arts and Commerce streams in 1985 and the construction of a modern three-storey building in 1999.

Today, the college serves over 1,650 students with a committed teaching staff, offering education in both Sinhala and Tamil mediums. Alongside academic excellence, students actively participate in sports and a wide range of co-curricular activities, achieving success at zonal, provincial, and national levels. Galle Muslim Ladies College proudly stands as the only Muslim girls’ school in the Southern Province.

At present, Galle Muslim Ladies College is progressing towards becoming a Smart School with modernized administrative and educational practices. In 2023, the school won first place in the Southern Province at the Smart School Competition.
